    Why KEN?

    According to a 2015 report by IHS on the installed base for video surveillance equipment, there is a camera installed for every 29 people on the planet, with mature markets like the US having a camera for every eight of its citizens. The report expects the number of cameras to grow by 20% year-over-year for the next five years. Video analytics from these cameras are used for traffic control, retail store monitoring, surveillance and security, as well as consumer applications including digital assistants for real-time decisions. There are many surveillance cameras that have been installed, but cannot be closely monitored throughout the day. Since events are more likely to occur while the operator is not watching, many significant events go undetected, even when they are recorded. Users can't be expected to trace through hours of video footage, especially if they're not sure what they're looking for.

    Our product aims to alleviate this problem by using deep reinforcement algorithms to emergency conditions in a video feed. These algorithms can be applied to both live streams and previously recorded video. This allows for an operator to invoke complex queries and run analytics against the collected data.

    Our product is focused on:

    1. Plug and play on top of existing camera infrastructure
    2. Both on-prem solution and cloud solution
    3. Deep-reinforcement AI for self learning
    4. Prioritise events according to criticality
    5. Centralised access
    6. Escalation matrix for emergency alerts
    7. Process video streams to find areas of interest compared to the manual search
    8. Periodic and Historic Reports on incidents
